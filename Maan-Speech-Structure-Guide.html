Audience: 85 attendees at fundraising Iftar
Length: 5-7 minutes (750-900 words)
Tone: Personal, vulnerable, hopeful, urgent
Goal: Move hearts to give tonight

  • you do not need to follow exactly, but this is a guide.

Speech Structure: The Live Hero’s Journey

OPENING (60 seconds): Hook & Stakes

Start with the dream—vividly. Make them feel it.

“Assalamu Alaikum. I’m going to tell you about the night I woke up at 3 AM in a cold sweat—not from a nightmare, but from a dream so vivid I knew it wasn’t from me. I saw children laughing in Arabic, their hands moving over Montessori letters, their hearts connected to Quran. I woke my husband and said, ‘I think Allah just gave me a job.’”

Pause. Look around the room.

“I was a stay-at-home mom of four. I had no business plan, no board, no building. Just a dream and a dua. But I knew one thing: Tampa Bay didn’t need another weekend memorization program. Our kids needed a place where Arabic was love, not a subject.”

THE LEAP (90 seconds): Vulnerability Builds Trust

Show them the risk. This makes your success more powerful.

“The next morning, I did something terrifying. I took our savings—money we’d set aside for a bigger car—and I signed a lease on an empty room. I didn’t know what Montessori training cost. I didn’t know how to register a non-profit. But I knew ‘معًا’—together—was the only way forward.”

Name the early believers. Make them feel included.

“I called one neighbor. She called two friends. Within a week, we had a board. Within a month, we had three students. Within a year, we had a waitlist. I want you to look around this room—every one of you who said ‘yes’ in those early days, you are the reason we’re here.”

THE PROOF (90 seconds): Show, Don’t Just Tell

This is where the dinner video plays. Reference it directly.

“While we eat, you’ll see faces that tell this story better than I can. You’ll hear a mother describe her son—who used to hide under the table during Arabic class—now begging to come on weekends. You’ll see a child, no older than seven, reading Quran with confidence because Montessori made it her discovery, not my demand.”

Connect to Islamic values.

“Every board meeting starts with Istikhara. Every success ends with Alhamdulillah. This isn’t how non-profits usually run. But this isn’t a usual non-profit. This is sadaqah jariyah you can see working every single weekend.”

THE URGENCY (60 seconds): Why Tonight Matters

Make the need tangible and immediate.

“Right now, we are turning families away. Not because they don’t want this. Because we have one room. Twenty kids. A waitlist of forty. A mother called me last week, crying, because she couldn’t get her daughter in. That is the sound of a dream outpacing its walls.”

Paint the future—specifically.

“Tonight, we’re not asking you to fund a dream. We’re asking you to fund a reality. A full-time program. A space where Arabic is the language of play, not just class. A place where ‘معًا’ means 200 families, not 20.”

THE ASK (60 seconds): Make Them the Hero

Direct, clear, and sacred.

“In a moment, you’ll see a QR code on your table. Before you leave tonight, I want you to do three things:”

Hold up three fingers. Count them down.

  1. "Give what you can." (Pause) “Not what you think is impressive. What you can. Allah multiplies intention, not amount.”
  2. "Share this story." (Pause) “One text message to a friend is sadaqah jariyah.”
  3. "Believe in ‘معًا’." (Pause) “This dream didn’t stop with me. It continues with you.”

CLOSING (30 seconds): End with Gratitude & Dua

Bring it back to the beginning. Full circle.

“That night I woke up at 3 AM, I wrote one word on a piece of paper: معًا. Tonight, you are writing the next chapter. Jazakum Allahu Khairan. May Allah multiply every gift and write this as ongoing charity for you and your families.”

Pause. Smile. Make eye contact with the room.

“BarakAllahu feekum.”

Speech Delivery Tips

Pacing:

  • Speak slower than feels natural (nerves speed you up)
  • Pause for 3 seconds after key lines (especially the dream, the ask)
  • Let the video breathe—don’t talk over it

Body Language:

  • Stand center stage, not behind a podium if possible
  • Use hand gestures when counting the three asks
  • Make eye contact with at least 5 different people in the audience

Tone:

  • Opening: Soft, intimate (like telling a secret)
  • Leap: Energetic, hopeful
  • Proof: Proud, confident
  • Urgency: Serious but not desperate
  • Ask: Clear, direct, unapologetic
  • Closing: Warm, grateful

What to Avoid:

  • ❌ Don’t apologize for asking for money
  • ❌ Don’t use statistics (they don’t move hearts)
  • ❌ Don’t speak longer than 7 minutes (attention drops)
  • ❌ Don’t read from paper (use bullet points only if needed)

What to Include:

  • ✅ Specific details (3 AM, three students, one room)
  • ✅ Names of early supporters (makes it personal)
  • ✅ The word “معًا” at least 5 times (repetition creates memory)
  • ✅ “Sadaqah jariyah” (frames donation as eternal investment)

Pre-Speech Checklist (Day Of)

  • Practice speech 3 times out loud (in front of mirror or family)
  • Test the dinner video playback with sound
  • Have a printed copy of the three asks on your table (backup)
  • Identify 3-5 friendly faces in audience to make eye contact with
  • Breathe deeply before walking on stage (calms nerves)

Remember: Your story is powerful because it’s true. You don’t need to be a professional speaker—you need to be authentic. The audience is already on your side. They’re here to support you. Now show them why their support matters.
